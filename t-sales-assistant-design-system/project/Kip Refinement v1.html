01 / Analysis

What to keep, what to refine, what to drop.

Read of the current sheet against the stated brief: operator-first, sales-floor ready, scales across surfaces.

Keep · non-negotiable

  • Glossy black visor with magenta eye glyphs The single strongest identity marker. Works at 16px and 500px.
  • Eye-as-expression system (8 states) Readable, memorable, animatable, licensable. Keep the set locked.
  • Helmet + utility jacket silhouette Reads as "operator" not "mascot" — critical for sales credibility.
  • Motto: "Knowledge strengthens the signal." Ties character, product, and role in one line. Keep as voice north star.
  • Kip as holographic & physical presence The hologram stance is the bridge to the runner game. Preserve.

Refine · ships better

  • Material consistency across expressions Helmet shading drifts between cells. Lock one PBR spec (matte graphite + chrome rim + gloss visor).
  • Eye glow intensity Some frames burn out highlights. Cap bloom radius; expose as --eye-glow token.
  • Expression naming collisions "Helpful" and "Happy" read similar at small sizes. Either redesign Helpful or rename Happy → Friendly.
  • 360° pose consistency Slight height/stance drift between views. Re-shoot from locked rig before final export.
  • Color palette documentation Swatches shown but not tokenized. Emit real tokens with contrast pairs (below).
  • "Kip in Action" hologram effect Pretty, but not spec'd. Define particle count, ring count, color ramp, frame rate budget.

Drop or defer

  • Generic "chatbot bubble" appearances Kip is a sidekick, not an agent. Never render inside a speech bubble in-product.
  • Satellite / Starlink iconography in-UI Fine on the poster; feels off in-product. Demote to onboarding art only.
  • Multiple full-body renders in live UI One orb + one full-figure ceiling per screen. Don't repeat the character.
  • Kip as decorative filler If a screen doesn't need a recommendation, Kip shouldn't appear. Presence must earn attention.
02 / Visual System

Material, light, and color — locked and tokenized.

The sheet reads beautifully as a poster but isn't engineerable yet. These tokens turn the character language into implementation-ready constants.

Helmet + Visor · material spec

Helmet dome: matte graphite (#2a2530 → #4a4652 vertical), chrome rim at 1.5% edge width, soft top highlight at 12% opacity max.

Visor glass: gloss black with single top-left specular, magenta rim-glow at 0.5px stroke. No internal reflections of environment — visor is read as a display, not a mirror.

Ear pads: same graphite family, no accent lighting. They terminate the silhouette; let them stay quiet.

Lighting · three-point rule

Key (magenta): from camera-right, 30° above, intensity 1.0 — creates the signature rim.

Fill (violet): from camera-left, low, intensity 0.3 — separates helmet from dark environments.

Ambient (neutral): 0.1 max — keep the silhouette readable against any backdrop without washing the magenta.

Cap bloom: 12px at 1x, 24px at 2x. Anything above burns the visor glyph.

Color tokens

All accents share chroma within 0.02 of each other so they sit in one family. Magenta carries identity; violet and cyan are reserved for runner/game only.

03 / Animation States

Six states. Every transition under 240ms.

The sheet covers expressions but not motion. These are the six states Kip cycles through on the sales floor — each with a timing spec so they feel consistent whether they're rendered in CSS (orb) or in After Effects (runner).

01 Idle
Soft breath-pulse on eyes. Default state whenever Kip is present but not needed.
4000ms · ease-in-out · loop
02 Listening
Eye-blink on each transcript chunk. Signals "I'm tracking the call."
1400ms · trigger on STT chunk
03 Thinking
Horizontal dot-shimmer. Shown only if response takes >400ms — otherwise skip.
1800ms · loop until resolved
04 Alert
Eye color shifts to amber, slow blink. Compliance risk or objection detected.
800ms · loop · amber #FFBC5C
05 Celebrate
Star-burst eyes, 600ms bounce. Fires once on close/win, never on generic activity.
600ms · once · no loop
06 Assist-mode
50% opacity, frozen eyes. Kip visible but stepped back — rep is driving, Kip is reference-only.
fade · 240ms · ease-out

One animation rule above all

Kip never animates during customer speech except for the Listening blink. Motion during the customer's turn pulls rep attention away from the caller — the cardinal sin.

04 / UX Role

Three presence levels. Earn every pixel.

Kip is not an always-on chat window. He's a tiered presence that expands only when the situation requires attention. Three levels, each with clear trigger rules.

LVL 01 · Pin

Dormant · 44×44

Voice rules — the short list

Always

• Lead with the action, not the explanation
• One recommendation per suggestion, never two
• Give the rep an exact phrase they can say
• Confidence labels where data supports it (82% match)
• End with a clear next-step button

Never

• "Great question!" / "I'd be happy to help" / "Let me explain"
• Longer than two sentences in Whisper or auto-Panel
• Animate during customer speech (except Listening blink)
• Appear on a screen where no recommendation is warranted
• Use first-person plural ("we recommend") — Kip speaks as himself

What changed vs. today

Kip lives only in the rail. No floating avatar, no bubble over the transcript, no full-body render in the live view. The full character is reserved for runner/training/onboarding.

Why one rail, not a floater

Reps look at two things during a call: the customer info and the transcript. A rail is read-in-peripheral; a floater steals central attention. Rail wins on eye-tracking alone.

Responsive rules

Desktop: rail docked, 320px.
Tablet: rail collapses to Whisper + Summon button.
Mobile (in-store handhelds): Pin only, bottom-right; tap for sheet.

07 / Runner Handoff

Clean transition to the game layer.

Light integration only. The same character, the same eye language, dropped into the runner context without rebuilding.

RUNNER · Kip · training loop

Transition rule

From sales-floor → runner: Kip's orb expands into full figure via a 400ms "beam-up" transition. Same visor, same eye state, no crossfade. Eyes stay on determined.

What stays consistent

Eye color (magenta), helmet silhouette, lighting key-angle, and all 8 named expressions. The environment changes, not the character.

What game adds

Violet accent (track horizon only), jetpack trail particles, speed-line post FX. None of these leak back into the sales-floor UI.
