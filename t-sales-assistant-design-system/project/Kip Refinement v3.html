Reset from v2

v2 over-corrected — Kip got reduced to a docked rail. v3 puts him back where he belongs: woven into every screen, with five distinct presence modes that scale from a 16px chip to a full-character takeover. The character does the work; the UI gets out of his way.

01 / Principles

Four rules for an ambient sidekick.

Borrowed from Siri / Alexa / Clippy lessons — refined for a sales floor where the rep can't afford a single bad interruption.

01

Always present, rarely loud

Kip's chip lives in the global chrome of every screen — like a status bar. He's there. He's reachable. He doesn't shout.

02

Earn the pixel

Each surface has a budget: chip · whisper · card · panel · takeover. Kip only escalates when the moment justifies it.

03

Character is the interaction

The visor expressions, eye color shifts, and idle pulse are the UI feedback. No spinners. No toast notifications. Kip reacts.

04

One Kip, one signal

Never two recommendations on screen. Never two visors. If Kip is on full-takeover, his chip in the chrome dims. One Kip, one ask.

02 / Presence Ladder

Five sizes of Kip — one personality.

The same character, scaled and styled for whatever the moment needs. Kip can be a 16-pixel chip in the chrome and a full hero figure in onboarding — same eyes, same voice, same trust.

01 · Chip

Always-on

Lives in the global chrome. Tap to summon, glance to know he's tracking. 16–24px.

02 · Whisper

One-line nudge

Inline cameo + ≤ 40 chars. Auto-dismisses in 6s. Never blocks.

03 · Card

Action with reasoning

Suggestion + 1–2 buttons + a why. Drops in-flow, doesn't take over.

04 · Panel

Dedicated companion

Right-rail in live call · sidebar in training. Persistent during the task.

05 · Takeover

Full character

Hero figure. Onboarding · post-close celebration · runner mini-game · daily morale.

09 / Density Budget

Kip on every screen — under 8% of pixels.

The trick to "everywhere, never overloading" is a strict pixel budget. Each screen has a max Kip footprint. Go over it, and Kip starts feeling like a chatbot. Stay under it, and he feels like a teammate.

Per-screen budget

Max footprint: 8% of viewport pixels at any moment.
Persistent chip: always counts ~1.5%.
Whisper: ~3% for ≤ 6 seconds.
Card: ~6% sustained.
Panel: 18-22% — only on dedicated assist screens.

One Kip rule

Never two Kip surfaces above Whisper at once. If a Card is up, the chip dims. If a Takeover fires, everything else collapses. The rep should never have to ask "which Kip am I talking to."

Cooldowns

Card → Card: 12s minimum gap.
Inline note → Inline note: 8s.
Takeover → next Takeover: 90s.
Compliance alerts ignore cooldowns — they always fire.
